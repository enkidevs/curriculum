## Content

If the path starts and ends with the same vertex, having more than one edge, it is called a **cycle**. A **directed cycle** is equivalent to a *directed path*. The cycle below is `[C, E, A]` (note: every permutation that follows the edge directions is a valid cycle - `[E, A, C]` or `[A, C, E, A, C]`).

A *path* is **simple** if the it contains each edge and vertex at most *once*. If a *simple path* ends with the vertex it started, it becomes a **simple cycle**.

Given two connected vertices of an *undirected graph*, *v* and *u*, we say that *u* **reaches** *v* and that *v* is **reachable** from *u*. In the case of a *directed graph*, one might be true, while the other might not.

A **directed graph** is **strongly connected** if for any two vertices, *v* reaches *u* and *u* reaches *v*. In case either one of `(B,E)` or `(E,B)` edges wasn't there, *B* wouldn't have been part of the strongly connected component anymore.
